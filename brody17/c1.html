Introduction to Centre

The Centre control position is part of what is known as an ARTCC: An Air Route Traffic Control Centre. There are many ARTCCs across the United States, each responsible for hundreds of miles of airspace at a time.

Centre controllers are responsible for taking aircraft from TRACON controllers, climbing them to their cruise altitude, turning them onto the correct course, and once there, making sure all aircraft stay far enough away from each other, to ensure proper separation.

Centre controllers are considered to have the hardest job of all control positions, dealing with the most traffic at a time, and the most airspace. In addition to this, centre controllers must be able to work any other control position, for the entire airspace. In the event an aircraft calls from an uncontrolled airfield, the centre controller must handle all positions for this aircraft, from Clearance Delivery, all the way to Centre.
