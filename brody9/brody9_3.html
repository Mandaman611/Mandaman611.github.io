History of the United States Navy

The 1700's

  • 1770-1799
    • June 12, 1775
      • The Rhode Island Navy is comissioned by the Rhode Island assembly.
      • The armed ships are among the first to actively fight against the British.
    • October 13, 1775
      • The Continental Congress establishes the Continental Navy.
    • September 23, 1779
      • The Battle of Flamborough Head
      • Commander John Paul Jones of the Bonhomme Richard wins a naval battle against the warship HMS Serapis.
  • 1780-1789
    • 1783
      • The Treaty of Paris ends the Revolutionary War, and seeing no need for a standing Navy, Congress disbands the Navy
  • 1790-1799
    • March 27,1794
      • Piracy and aggression by other nations leads to the Naval Act of 1794.
      • Six ships, including the USS Constitution are created.
    • 1798-1800
      • The Quasi-War
      • An undeclared war breaks out, fought only on the seas of France.
    • April 30, 1798
      • Creation of the Department of the Navy
