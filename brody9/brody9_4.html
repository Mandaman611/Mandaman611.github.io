History of the United States Navy

The 1800's

  • 1800-1809
    • 1801-1805
      • Rogue states from the North African Coast commit acts of piracy in the Mediterranean sea.
      • President Jefferson sends the Navy and Marines, who engage directly with pirates.
      • The Navy re-opens safe shipping lanes.
    • June 22, 1807
      • The HMS Leopard boards and captures the USS Chesapeake looking for British Navy deserters.
      • USS Chesapeake returns later to fight in the War of 1812.
  • 1810-1829
    • 1812-1815
      • The War of 1812 begins.
      • The British Royal Navy outnumbers the US Navy nearly 50:1.
      • American ships win many battles.
      • The US harasses British ships and commerce all around the world.
    • August 19,1812
      • The USS Constitution engages the HMS Guerriere.
      • British cannonballs bounce off the hull of the Constitution.
    • June 1, 1813
      • The USS Chesapeake captures the HMS Shannon.
    • September 10,1813
      • The Navy wins the Battle of Lake Erie.
      • This forces the British to cede control of Detroit and the Great Lakes.
      • About a quarter of the American naval personnel were African American.
    • May 20-July 3, 1815
      • The Barbary pirates begin raiding the Mediterranean again.
      • The Navy dispatches two ships, who fight the pirates until they release all captives and ransoms taken.
  • 1840-1849
    • 1846-1848
      • Mexican American War
      • Navy blockaded Mexico and helped to capture Califonia
    • March 9-29, 1847
      • The Battle of Veracruz
      • The Navy lands 12,000 troops on a Mexican beachhead.
      • Led to the capture of Mexico City and US Victory
  • 1860-1869
    • 1861-1865
      • The Civil War
    • March 8, 1862
      • First battle between two ironclad ships.
      • USS Monitor fights the CSS Virginia to a standstill.
    • February 17, 1864
      • The first American submarine, CSS Hunley sinks the USS Housatonic using an underwater torpedo.
  • 1890-1899
    • February 15,1898
      • The USS Maine explodes in Havana Harbor.
      • The Spanish-American war begins.
