About Us

We are women who are tired of yielding to men. We have created this website for people to learn more about our cause and to recruit both women and men who believe in our cause.

About Our Staff

We are the Women's Rights organization... Meet our staff.

The following is a list of women who are fighting beside us for women’s rights in their own ways.

Susan B Anthony

is a temperance activist and is currently fighting for many rights, one of which is women’s right to vote.

Emma Willard

opened one of the nation’s first academically challenging schools called Troy Female Seminary which is located in Troy, New York.

Elizabeth Blackwell

is the first woman to graduate from medical school. She opened the New York Infirmary for Women and Children.

Sojourner Truth

is a free slave who fights and speaks on behalf of abolition.

Sarah and Angelina Grimké

are speakers for abolition. Angelina Grimké has published a book called An Appeal to Christian Women of the South. In the book, Grimké calls on women to overthrow the oppression they face. Both sisters run schools for women and, in Letters on the Equality of the Sexes and the Condition of Woman, they address the lack of education women recieve.

Elizabeth Cady Stanton and Lucretia Mott

organized the first women’s rights convention in Seneca Falls.

About our Cause

We are currently fighting for women to have equal rights as men.

In particular, we are fighting for higher education for women. It is time to allow women to have the same education as men and to be able to further their knowledge. There are currently academically rigorous schools open to women such as Troy Female Seminary which are facing much opposition. In addition, we want to assure that women have the right to vote, to own property, and to fight against the evils of slavery.

As women, we also want to address the temperance movement. Alcohol is a dangerous substance that gains control of men and makes them act abusively. Drunkenness is a problem. We are the poor wives of these drunkards and the victims of their actions. Support our fight to limit alcohol! Overall, to join our cause, you can help in many ways such as raising money, passing out literature, and collecting signatures for our petitions to Congress.
